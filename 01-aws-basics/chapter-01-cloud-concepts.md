# Module 1, Chapter 1: Cloud Concepts & Value Proposition

## 🧠 Part 1: Core Theory

Cloud computing is the on-demand delivery of IT resources over the internet with pay-as-you-go pricing. It allows you to stop thinking of technology as a physical product (servers) and start seeing it as a utility (like water or electricity).

### 1. The 6 Advantages of Cloud
1. **Trade Capital Expense (CapEx) for Variable Expense (OpEx)**: Pay only when you consume resources.
2. **Benefit from Massive Economies of Scale**: AWS buys in bulk, so you pay less.
3. **Stop Guessing Capacity**: Scale up when busy, scale down when quiet.
4. **Increase Speed and Agility**: Launch resources in minutes.
5. **Stop Spending Money Running Data Centers**: Focus on customers, not hardware.
6. **Go Global in Minutes**: Deploy worldwide instantly.

## 🎙️ Part 3: Top 10 Priority Interview Q&A

| # | Question | Answer |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | **What is Cloud Computing?** | The on-demand delivery of IT resources via the internet with pay-as-you-go pricing. |
| 2 | **What is "Agility" in AWS?** | The ability to quickly develop, test, and launch applications. |
| 3 | **What is "Elasticity"?** | The power to scale resources up and down based on demand automatically. |
| 4 | **Difference between Scalability and Elasticity?** | Scalability handles long-term growth; Elasticity handles short-term spikes. |
| 5 | **What is a "Consumption-based" model?** | A model where you pay only for what you use (no upfront costs). |
| 6 | **What are "Economies of Scale"?** | Cost savings achieved by AWS due to large-scale operations, passed to you. |
| 7 | **What is "High Availability"?** | Designing a system to be accessible nearly all the time with no single point of failure. |
| 8 | **What is "Fault Tolerance"?** | The ability of a system to stay up even if a major component fails completely. |
| 9 | **What is CapEx?** | Capital Expenditure: Money spent upfront on physical assets. |
| 10 | **What is OpEx?** | Operating Expenditure: Ongoing costs to run a business (like your AWS bill). |
